"We need to call the police," said Dad.

"But there's no phone signal," said Finn.

"I'll use the radio in the lighthouse," said Dad. "You stay here. Lock the door."

Dad left.

Five minutes later, Finn heard voices outside.

He looked out the window.

Two people were near the lighthouse! One woman. One man. They were looking at the rocks.

Finn got Dad's binoculars.

The woman had grey hair. The man had a red beard. They had clipboards and were writing things down.

Finn crept outside. He wanted to see what they were doing. He hid behind a rock.

"We've been searching for months," said the woman. "The old monastery maps show it should be here."

"But we need to be careful," said the man. "We don't want to disturb the lighthouse keeper and his son."

"You're right," said the woman. "Let's wait until we can explain properly. This discovery is too important to rush."

Finn was curious. What were they looking for?

He leaned forward to hear better.

His foot hit a small rock. Clatter!

"Oh! Someone's there!" said the woman. She didn't sound angry. Just surprised.

Finn stood up. "Sorry! I didn't mean to spy!"

The woman smiled. "That's alright. You must be the lighthouse keeper's son?"

Just then, Dad came running out of the lighthouse. He'd heard the noise.

"Finn! Are you okay?" Then he saw the two people. "Who are you?"

"I'm Professor Winters," said the woman. "This is my assistant, Tom. We're archaeologists. We're very sorry for worrying you. Can we explain?"

🔧 Today's Activities

⏱️ Time: 20 minutes

🔍 Be an Archaeologist!

The Challenge: The people on the island are archaeologists! They look for old things. Let's be archaeologists too!

ACTIVITY 1: Make Binoculars

What You Need:

  • 2 toilet roll tubes
  • Tape
  • String
  • Black paper

How to Make:

  • Put tubes side by side
  • Tape them together
  • Wrap in black paper
  • Make two small holes (ask an adult)
  • Put string through
  • Wear them!

ACTIVITY 2: Archaeological Dig

What You Need:

  • A tray or box
  • Sand or rice
  • Small toys or objects to "bury"
  • A spoon (your digging tool!)
  • Paper and pencil

How to Play:

Set Up:

  • Fill tray with sand or rice
  • Hide 5-10 small objects in it
  • Smooth the top

Dig:

  • Use the spoon carefully
  • Dig slowly
  • When you find something, stop!
  • Draw what you found
  • Write down where you found it

What to Bury:

  • Coins
  • Small toys
  • Buttons
  • Shells
  • Plastic animals
  • Lego pieces

ACTIVITY 3: Make an Archaeologist Kit

What You Need:

  • Small bag or box
  • Notebook
  • Pencil
  • Ruler
  • Magnifying glass (if you have one)
  • Your binoculars

Label it: "Archaeologist Kit"

ACTIVITY 4: Draw What You Find

When archaeologists find things, they draw them!

Practice:

  • Find 3 old things in your house (old coin, old toy, old button)
  • Draw each one carefully
  • Write where you found it
  • How old do you think it is?

📚 Research Activities

RESEARCH 1: What is an Archaeologist?

Questions to Find Out:

1. What do archaeologists do?

  • Look it up online with an adult
  • Watch a video about archaeologists
  • Draw three things they do

2. What tools do they use?

  • Small brushes
  • Trowels (small shovels)
  • Sifting screens
  • Measuring tapes
  • Cameras
  • Draw or find pictures of these tools

3. Where do they work?

  • Deserts
  • Jungles
  • Under the sea
  • In caves
  • In cities
  • Find photos of different dig sites

RESEARCH 2: Famous Archaeological Discoveries

Find out about:

1. Tutankhamun's Tomb (Egypt)

  • When: 1922
  • Who found it: Howard Carter
  • What they found: Golden mask, treasure, mummy
  • Look at pictures online
  • Draw the golden mask

2. Pompeii (Italy)

  • When: 1748 (but buried in 79 AD)
  • What happened: Volcano buried the city
  • What they found: Whole Roman city preserved
  • Look at pictures of Pompeii
  • Draw a Roman building

3. Terracotta Army (China)

  • When: 1974
  • What they found: Thousands of clay soldier statues
  • Why: To guard an emperor's tomb
  • Look at pictures
  • Count how many soldiers you can see in one photo!

4. Dinosaur Fossils

  • Where: All over the world
  • What: Bones that turned to stone
  • Famous ones: T-Rex, Triceratops
  • Draw your favorite dinosaur

RESEARCH 3: Visit a Museum (Or Look Online)

With an adult:

If you can visit a real museum:

  • Go to a local museum
  • Look for old things
  • Draw your three favorite objects
  • Read the labels
  • How old are they?

If you look at a museum online:

  • British Museum (London) - has virtual tours
  • Natural History Museum - see dinosaurs
  • Look at their websites with an adult
  • Find the oldest thing you can see
  • How old is it?
