Mystery at Blackrock Lighthouse

⏱️ 5 min read

📖 Reading Timer (Optional)
00:00

Finn looked out of the car window. Rain was running down the glass. Through the rain, he could see it - Blackrock Lighthouse! It was tall and white with red stripes. It stood on a rocky island in the sea.

"There it is!" Finn shouted. He pointed at the lighthouse.

His dad smiled. "That's where we're going to stay for two weeks. I need to fix the light at the top. Do you like it?"

Finn nodded. "It's amazing!" He was ten years old. This was so exciting! His dad fixed lighthouses for his job. Usually Finn stayed with Granny when Dad went away for work. But this time, Dad said Finn could come too!

"We need to drive across this road quickly," said Dad. He looked at his watch. "When the sea gets high, the water will cover the road. Then we'll be stuck on the island for twelve hours!"

They drove across a very thin road. Finn looked down. Water was on both sides! Big waves crashed on the rocks. Splash! Water flew up into the air. It was scary but fun.

The lighthouse got bigger and bigger as they drove closer. It was huge! At the bottom was a small house where people used to live when they looked after the lighthouse.

"Get your bag," said Dad as they parked the car. "Let's get inside before we get too wet!"

The house was cold. It smelled like salt water and old wood. Finn looked around. There were three little bedrooms, a kitchen with an old cooker, and a living room. The living room had a fireplace and lots of old photos on the walls. The photos showed people who used to work at the lighthouse a long time ago.

"Nobody has lived here for twenty years," Dad said. He started making a fire. "The lighthouse works by itself now. But the light needs fixing, so they asked me to come and do it."

That evening, after they ate soup and sandwiches, Dad went up the lighthouse. There were 127 steps to the top! Finn stayed in the house. He sat by the fire and read his book. But he kept hearing the wind howling outside. Whooooo! And waves crashing. CRASH!

At nine o'clock, Dad came back down. He looked worried.

"That's strange," he said quietly.

"What's wrong?" asked Finn.

"Someone has been up there. Very recently. I can see footprints in the dust. And someone moved my tools."

"But nobody else is here," said Finn. "Right?"

Dad looked serious. "Nobody should be here. This lighthouse is closed. We're the only people allowed on the island."

CRASH! A loud noise came from outside. Both of them jumped. Dad grabbed a torch and went to the door. "Stay here," he said.

But Finn followed Dad outside anyway. He wanted to see what the noise was! The torch light moved across the ground. At first, they couldn't see anything. Just rain and wind. Then the light showed something that made them both stop and stare.

The door to the lighthouse tower was wide open! It was swinging in the wind. Bang! Bang! But Dad had locked that door. Finn was sure of it.

🔧 Today's Activity

⏱️ Time: 15-20 minutes

🏗️ Engineer Your Lighthouse

The Challenge: Finn's dad is a lighthouse engineer who repairs and maintains lighthouses. Your job is to build a model lighthouse that can actually light up!

📦 What You'll Need:

Basic materials:

  • Empty cardboard tube (kitchen roll, wrapping paper tube, or Pringles can)
  • White paper or paint
  • Red paper, marker, or paint (for the stripes)
  • Small torch or LED tea light
  • Scissors and glue/tape
  • Cardboard for the base

Optional extras:

  • Tin foil (for a reflective lamp room)
  • Clear plastic cup (for the lamp room dome)
  • Black card (for windows)
  • Small rocks or playdough (to create the rocky island base)

🔨 Instructions:

1. Create the lighthouse tower:

  • Cover your cardboard tube with white paper
  • Add red spiral stripes like Blackrock Lighthouse
  • Cut out small black windows going up the tower

2. Build the lamp room at the top:

  • Cut a circle of cardboard slightly larger than your tube
  • Make small cuts around the edge and fold up to create a rim
  • Line the inside with tin foil to reflect light
  • Place your torch or LED light inside
  • If you have a clear plastic cup, cut it to fit as a dome on top

3. Create the base:

  • Cut a large circle of cardboard for the island
  • Glue your lighthouse tube to the center
  • Add grey/brown paint or paper for rocks
  • Optional: use playdough or stones to make it 3D

4. Add details:

  • Draw or create a small cottage at the base
  • Make a thin strip of card for the causeway connecting to "mainland"
  • Add blue paper or paint around the island for water

5. Test your light:

  • Turn on your torch/LED light in the lamp room
  • Does it shine through the top?
  • Take it into a dark room - how far can you see the light?

🤔 Engineering Challenge Questions:

After building, discuss:

  • Why do you think lighthouses are so tall?
    Think about: How far the light needs to shine, ships seeing it from a distance
  • Why is Blackrock Lighthouse striped?
    Think about: Ships needing to identify which lighthouse they're seeing during the day
  • What makes a good lighthouse location?
    Think about: Rocky coasts, dangerous areas for ships, visibility
  • Why did they paint lighthouses different patterns?
    Research: Each lighthouse has a unique "daymark" so sailors can identify it

📸 Photo Challenge:

Once you've built your lighthouse:

  • Take a photo of it in daylight
  • Take a photo of it in a dark room with the light on
  • Compare the two photos - which shows the lighthouse's purpose better?

💡 Did You Know?

  • The tallest lighthouse in the world is Jeddah Light in Saudi Arabia (133 meters tall!)
  • The oldest working lighthouse is the Tower of Hercules in Spain (built by the Romans around 100 AD!)
  • Before electricity, lighthouses used oil lamps and huge mirrors to magnify the light
  • Lighthouse keepers had to wind up clockwork mechanisms that rotated the light - just like Finn's dad is repairing!
