Mystery at Blackrock Lighthouse

Finn looked out of the car window. It was raining.

He could see the lighthouse! It was big and white. It had red stripes. It was on an island.

"There it is!" Finn shouted.

Dad smiled. "That's where we will stay. For two weeks. I need to fix the light. Do you like it?"

Finn nodded. "Yes!" He was ten years old. This was so exciting!

Dad fixed lighthouses. Usually Finn stayed with Granny. But this time, Dad said Finn could come too!

"We must cross this road quickly," said Dad. "When the sea gets high, the water will cover the road."

They drove across a thin road. Water was on both sides! Big waves splashed on the rocks.

The lighthouse got bigger. It was huge! At the bottom was a small house.

"Get your bag," said Dad. "Let's go inside."

The house was cold. It smelled like salt and old wood.

Finn looked around. There were three bedrooms. A kitchen. A living room with a fire. Old photos were on the walls.

"Nobody has lived here for twenty years," Dad said. He lit the fire. "The light needs fixing. I will fix it."

That evening, they ate soup. Then Dad went up the lighthouse. There were 127 steps!

Finn stayed in the house. He read his book. But he kept hearing the wind. Whooooo! And the waves. CRASH!

At nine o'clock, Dad came back. He looked worried.

"That's odd," he said.

"What's wrong?" asked Finn.

"Someone has been up there. There are footprints in the dust. Someone moved my tools."

"But nobody else is here," said Finn. "Right?"

Dad looked serious. "Nobody should be here. We are the only people on the island."

CRASH! A loud noise came from outside.

Dad grabbed a torch. "Stay here," he said.

But Finn followed. He wanted to see!

The torch light moved across the ground. They saw rain. They saw wind.

Then the light showed something.

The lighthouse door was open! It was swinging in the wind. Bang! Bang!

But Dad had locked that door. Finn was sure.

üèóÔ∏è Engineer Your Lighthouse

The Challenge: Finn's dad is a lighthouse engineer who repairs and maintains lighthouses. Your job is to build a model lighthouse that can actually light up!

üì¶ What You'll Need:

Basic materials:

  • Empty cardboard tube (kitchen roll, wrapping paper tube, or Pringles can)
  • White paper or paint
  • Red paper, marker, or paint (for the stripes)
  • Small torch or LED tea light
  • Scissors and glue/tape
  • Cardboard for the base

Optional extras:

  • Tin foil (for a reflective lamp room)
  • Clear plastic cup (for the lamp room dome)
  • Black card (for windows)
  • Small rocks or playdough (to create the rocky island base)

üî® Instructions:

1. Create the lighthouse tower:

  • Cover your cardboard tube with white paper
  • Add red spiral stripes like Blackrock Lighthouse
  • Cut out small black windows going up the tower

2. Build the lamp room at the top:

  • Cut a circle of cardboard slightly larger than your tube
  • Make small cuts around the edge and fold up to create a rim
  • Line the inside with tin foil to reflect light
  • Place your torch or LED light inside
  • If you have a clear plastic cup, cut it to fit as a dome on top

3. Create the base:

  • Cut a large circle of cardboard for the island
  • Glue your lighthouse tube to the center
  • Add grey/brown paint or paper for rocks
  • Optional: use playdough or stones to make it 3D

4. Add details:

  • Draw or create a small cottage at the base
  • Make a thin strip of card for the causeway connecting to "mainland"
  • Add blue paper or paint around the island for water

5. Test your light:

  • Turn on your torch/LED light in the lamp room
  • Does it shine through the top?
  • Take it into a dark room - how far can you see the light?

ü§î Engineering Challenge Questions:

After building, discuss:

  • Why do you think lighthouses are so tall?
    Think about: How far the light needs to shine, ships seeing it from a distance
  • Why is Blackrock Lighthouse striped?
    Think about: Ships needing to identify which lighthouse they're seeing during the day
  • What makes a good lighthouse location?
    Think about: Rocky coasts, dangerous areas for ships, visibility
  • Why did they paint lighthouses different patterns?
    Research: Each lighthouse has a unique "daymark" so sailors can identify it

üì∏ Photo Challenge:

Once you've built your lighthouse:

  • Take a photo of it in daylight
  • Take a photo of it in a dark room with the light on
  • Compare the two photos - which shows the lighthouse's purpose better?

üí° Did You Know?

  • The tallest lighthouse in the world is Jeddah Light in Saudi Arabia (133 meters tall!)
  • The oldest working lighthouse is the Tower of Hercules in Spain (built by the Romans around 100 AD!)
  • Before electricity, lighthouses used oil lamps and huge mirrors to magnify the light
  • Lighthouse keepers had to wind up clockwork mechanisms that rotated the light - just like Finn's dad is repairing!
