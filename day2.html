The Hidden Room

⏱️ 8 min read

📖 Reading Timer (Optional)
00:00

Dad walked to the lighthouse door. He shone his torch inside. The stairs went up into the dark.

"Hello?" he called. "Is anyone here?"

Nobody said anything. Only the wind went whooooo!

"This is odd," said Dad. "I locked this door. I used a key."

"Maybe the wind did it?" said Finn. But he knew that was not true.

Dad looked at the lock. "No. Someone opened this. Someone clever."

They went inside the lighthouse. They went up all 127 steps. Up and up and up to the very top. But they could not find anyone.

"Let's go to bed," said Dad. He locked the door again. Then he put a chair by it. "Time to sleep."

But Finn could not sleep. Who had been here? What did they want? How did they get here? The road was under water!

At midnight, Finn heard a sound.

Scrape... scrape...

It came from under his room. But there was nothing under his room. Just rock!

Finn got his torch. He got out of bed. He went into the hall. Dad's door was shut. Dad was asleep. Finn could hear him snoring.

Should he wake Dad?

Scrape... scrape...

There it was again!

Finn went downstairs. The stairs went creeeak! The room was dark. The fire was nearly out. Finn shone his torch.

Scrape...

The old rug by the fire was moving!

Finn was scared. His hands shook. He pulled the rug back.

Under the rug was a door in the floor! It was open a tiny bit.

Finn's torch showed steps. Stone steps going down. Cold air came up. It smelled wet. There were muddy footprints on the steps!

"Dad!" Finn called. "DAD!"

Dad ran down. "What is it?"

"Look! A door in the floor! And steps! Someone went down there!"

Dad looked. "Some old lighthouses have rooms under the ground. To keep things in. But I did not know about this one."

"Should we go down?" asked Finn.

Dad said, "Yes. But I go first. You stay close."

They went down the steps. Their torches made light. The steps went down and down and down.

At the bottom was a room. It had rock walls. A low roof. In the room was:

* Some old boxes
* A sleeping bag
* A little stove
* Some food

"Someone lives here," said Dad.

Then they saw more. A tunnel! It went into the rock. And on the wall was a note:

STOP ASKING QUESTIONS. LEAVE THE ISLAND. THIS IS YOUR ONLY WARNING.

Who wrote that? Why did they want Finn and Dad to go away?

🔧 Today's Activity

⏱️ Time: 20-25 minutes

🏗️ Build Your Own Secret Underground Room

The Challenge: In the story, Finn discovers a hidden trapdoor under a rug that leads to a secret underground room! Your job is to create your own secret trapdoor model with a hidden room beneath it.

📦 What You'll Need:

Basic materials:

  • A shoebox (with lid)
  • Scissors
  • Glue or tape
  • Brown paper or paint (for the floor)
  • A small piece of fabric or felt (for the rug)
  • Cardboard scraps
  • Markers or crayons
  • A torch (to shine through from underneath)

Optional extras:

  • Small toy figures (for Finn and Dad)
  • Cotton wool (for cobwebs)
  • Aluminum foil (for the stone walls)
  • Small pieces of sponge or foam (for the sleeping bag)
  • Tiny pebbles or sand (for the underground floor)
  • Lollipop sticks (for the ladder/steps)

🔨 Instructions:

1. Create the cottage floor (shoebox lid):

  • Turn the shoebox lid upside down to make the floor
  • Cover it with brown paper or paint it brown (like wooden floorboards)
  • Draw on some floorboard lines
  • Cut a small square trapdoor (about 5cm x 5cm) in one corner - DON'T cut it all the way! Leave one edge attached so it can open like a real door
  • Make the rug: Cut a piece of fabric/felt bigger than your trapdoor
  • Glue the rug over the trapdoor (but make sure the trapdoor can still lift up underneath!)

2. Build the underground room (inside the shoebox):

  • This is the secret room beneath the floor!
  • Paint the inside grey or cover with aluminum foil (for stone walls)
  • Use lollipop sticks to make a ladder going down from the trapdoor
  • Add texture with crumpled paper or foil for rocky walls

3. Add the secret room details:

  • Sleeping bag: Roll up a small piece of fabric or foam
  • Camping stove: Use a small bottle cap painted silver
  • Food supplies: Draw tiny boxes or use small beads
  • The tunnel: Cut a hole in one end of the box and make it dark inside
  • Warning note: Cut a tiny piece of paper and write "GO AWAY!" on it, stick it to the wall

4. Put it together:

  • Place the lid (with the trapdoor and rug) on top of the box
  • Make sure the trapdoor can lift up to reveal the secret room below!
  • Cut a small viewing window in the side of the box so you can see inside the secret room

5. Test your model:

  • Can you lift the rug to find the trapdoor?
  • Can you open the trapdoor to see the secret room?
  • Shine a torch through the viewing window - it should look spooky!

🔍 Mystery Detective Questions:

After building, discuss:

  • Why do you think someone would build a secret room?
    Think about: Hiding from danger, keeping treasures safe, secret meetings
  • Why was the trapdoor hidden under a rug?
    Think about: So nobody would see it, to keep it secret
  • How did Finn know someone had been in the secret room?
    Remember: Muddy footprints, camping equipment, fresh food
  • What clues would a detective look for in a secret room?
    Think about: Footprints, fingerprints, what's been moved, what's been left behind

📸 Photography Challenge:

Take photos of your model:

  • Closed trapdoor: With the rug covering it (can you see it?)
  • Open trapdoor: Looking down into the secret room
  • Inside view: Looking up from the secret room to the trapdoor
  • Dark photo: With just a torch shining in (spooky!)
