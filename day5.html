Mr. Castellan smiled. It was not a nice smile.

"Give me the books," he said.

"No," said Professor Winters. She held the box tight.

"They belong in a museum," said Dad. "Not in your house."

Mr. Castellan laughed. "Who will stop me? I have two big men. You have a child."

Finn felt scared. But then he had an idea.

"Dad," he whispered. "Your phone. Take a photo!"

Dad understood! He pulled out his phone. Click! He took a photo of Mr. Castellan.

"What are you doing?" said Mr. Castellan.

"Taking photos," said Dad. Click! Click! "Of you. And your men. And the books. If you take the books, I will show these photos to the police."

Mr. Castellan's face went red. "You can't prove anything!"

"Yes I can," said Professor Winters. She held up a piece of paper. "This is a letter. From the museum. It says we have permission to dig here. You don't. You are trespassing. That means you shouldn't be here."

"And," said Tom, "we called the police before we came down. They are coming right now. They will be here in ten minutes."

Mr. Castellan looked angry. Very angry. But he knew he was beaten.

"This isn't over," he said. He turned and walked back down the tunnel. His two men followed him.

Everyone was quiet. Then Professor Winters laughed!

"We did it!" she said. "We saved the books!"

They heard a noise from above. Voices!

"Hello? Anyone down there?"

"It's the police!" said Dad.

They all went up. Out into the daylight. Two police officers were there.

Dad told them everything. About Mr. Castellan. About the books. About the tunnel.

The police looked at the books. "These need to go to the museum right away," said one officer. "For safekeeping."

"Yes," said Professor Winters. "That's what we want."

The police took the books. They were very careful. They put them in a special box.

"You did a good thing," the police officer said to Finn. "You were brave. And you helped save something very special."

Finn felt proud.

Two days later, Finn and Dad went home. The lighthouse was fixed. The books were safe in the museum.

But before they left, Professor Winters came to say goodbye.

"Thank you," she said. "You saved history. These books will be in the museum. Children will learn from them. For hundreds of years. Because of you."

She gave Finn a present. It was a book. With pictures of the old manuscripts in it.

"So you can remember," she said.

Finn smiled. He would never forget his adventure at Blackrock Lighthouse.

The End
